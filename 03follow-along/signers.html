Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include

John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress, remembered for his large, bold signature (hence the phrase "put your John Hancock on it").
Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the Declaration, later the 3rd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
A leading statesman, inventor, and diplomat, often called "the First American."
John Adams
A strong advocate for independence, later the 2nd President of the United States.
Samuel Adams
A leader of the Sons of Liberty and cousin of John Adams, instrumental in organizing resistance against British rule.
Richard Henry Lee
The Virginian who formally proposed independence to Congress.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The only Catholic signer, and the longest-lived (died in 1832).
Roger Sherman
The only person to sign all four major founding documents: the Continental Association, the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Confederation, and the U.S. Constitution.

Each of these individuals played a significant role in the founding of the United States and is rememebred for their courage in signing the Declaration, an act considered treasonous by the British Crown.
