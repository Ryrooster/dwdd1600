Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship refers to the responsible and ethical use of technology, including understanding how to navigate the internet safely, respecting others online, and being aware of digital rights and responsibilities.

What is Digital Law?

Digital law refers to the legal framework governing the use of digital technology and the internet, including issues such as privacy, intellectual property, cybercrime, and online behavior.

What is AUP?

AUP stands for Acceptable Use Policy. It is a set of rules and guidelines that outline the acceptable use of technology and internet resources within an organization, such as a school or workplace.

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is the act of using someone else's work, ideas, or intellectual property without proper attribution or permission, presenting it as your own. It is considered unethical and can have serious consequences in academic and professional settings.

It doesn't matter whether it's done on purpose or by accident it's still plagiarism.

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty refers to any act of cheating or plagiarism in an academic setting, including but not limited to copying from others, using unauthorized materials during exams, or submitting work that is not your own.

In simple terms: it's cheating or being dishonest in schoolwork.

What are the rules for AI Generated Content?

AI-generated content should be used ethically and responsibly, with proper attribution when required. It's important to understand the limitations of AI and ensure that the content aligns with academic integrity standards.
